The origin

I got tired of watching people lose money on other people's opinions.

I've bet on college football. I've also lost money on it — early on, chasing gut feelings and half-built models that weren't ready. Being a CFO teaches you quickly that a bad model is worse than no model at all, because it gives you false confidence.

What I kept noticing around me was how many people were getting swept up in sports betting — and how consistently they were losing. Not because they weren't smart, but because they were making decisions based on instinct, narratives, and whoever they'd heard on a podcast that week. The sportsbook is very good at exploiting exactly that.

So I started asking whether an edge could actually be quantified. Over the last few years I've spent a lot of time — and a little bit of money — building, breaking, and refining models to find out. Nothing would make me happier than to see that work provide real value to the people out there who love wagering on sports but have never had a serious, data-driven source of analysis to lean on.

Transparency

What PressBox is not.

There are a lot of people selling picks on the internet. Most of them cherry-pick their wins, hide their losses, and charge a monthly fee for the privilege. PressBox is built on a different premise entirely.

✕
Not a tout service. Every pick we've ever made is published — wins, losses, pushes. Three full seasons. You can verify every number on the Results page yourself.
✕
Not a gambling addiction enabler. We publish one Pick of the Week and a small set of high-conviction plays. We're not trying to get you to bet every game every week.
✕
Not a black box. The How It Works page explains the methodology in plain English. You'll understand what we're measuring and why — just not the exact weights and thresholds.
✕
Not infallible. We lose weeks. We lose games. The model is wrong sometimes. What it isn't is consistently wrong — and over three seasons, that difference has added up.
