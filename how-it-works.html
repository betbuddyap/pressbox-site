03 — The grades

What each grade means.

Spreads and totals share one scale. Moneylines get their own three, because a longshot and a near-pickem are completely different bets. Every grade is a published verdict — including the ones that say "pass."

Spread & Total
A+Highest conviction
The cleanest setups we publish — a strong edge on a manageable number, with the model read and SP+ pulling the same way. Our most repeatable bet.
AStrong edge
A clear edge, just below A+ — strong model confirmation in profitable territory across the locked backtest, at lower volume.
No EdgeWe'll pass
We ran the game and didn't find an edge worth betting — or the market moved past the one we had. We publish it anyway, right on the board.
Moneyline
💰 Smart Money
Moneyline value at reasonable prices — near-pickem to modest favorites. The highest hit rate of the three ML grades.
🐻 Goldilocks
The middle-ground underdog — not chalk, not a longshot. Moderate dogs where the price and the read line up. Hits a little under half the time.
🎟 Lottery
Heavy underdogs and longshots. Hits less than a third of the time — but the payouts are big enough that the math works in your favor.

05 — The deep view

The game page.

Tap "full game breakdown" on any bet and you land on the deepest view on the site. It's where the grade gets explained — in writing, in the numbers, and in the model reads.

  • A written breakdown
    A plain-English read on the matchup — what the models see and why the bet landed where it did.
  • Every model's read
    All five models on one axis against the market line, for each market. More on this just below.
  • Team & advanced stats
    The numbers behind the call — efficiency, tempo, and the advanced splits the box score misses.
  • Head-to-head history
    How these two have met before.
  • Live scoring
    Once the game kicks off, the page tracks it in real time.

The model reads are the heart of it. Each model puts its number on one axis against the Vegas line. A bet fires when one model's read sits far enough off the line to meet a specific set of criteria — a proven combination of model, market, edge size, and line range. That firing model is the pick, shown in rust. When nothing meets the criteria, a single gold aggregate dot appears instead and the read grades No Edge.

06 — After the game

We're graded on the number we gave you.

When a bet releases, we freeze the line and tier we published. It re-grades against the live market every 30 minutes and can slip to No Edge as the line moves. When the game ends, win or loss is scored against the number we released — not a friendlier closing line. Every result lands on the Results page, win or lose.
